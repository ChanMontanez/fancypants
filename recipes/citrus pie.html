Citrus Pie

I love citrus. Truth be told, limes are my favorite, but I love all of them. This pie reminds me of lemon bars but with fruit slices added in. If you want a pie thats all orange, go for it! Lemon and lime? Do it up! Just make sure that the the amount, or more specifically the weight of fruit, is equal to the amount in the recipe.

If you dont want to make your own pie dough, don't. Plenty of companies make perfectly delicous pie crusts, so just pick your favorite. If you want to fancy this up with a lattice top, it would be lovely with the sunny color shining through. If you don't care for the upper crust, skip it. I'm all for making a recipe your own.

Citrus Pie

  • 2 medium-sized oranges, washed
  • 2 lemons, washed
  • 3 key limes, washed
  • 2 cups sugar
  • dash of salt
  • 4 large egg
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream
  • Homemade pie dough or a premade pie shell

Thinly grapefruit, orange, lemon and limes. The slices should be paper-thin, remove the seeds. Cut all but 3 slices of the lemon, 6 slices of the lime and 3 slices of the orange into eighths. Place the rings and slices into a plastic container, add the sugar and salt, mix gently, and refrigerate overnight.

On a lightly floured surface, roll out dough to 1/8 inch thick; drape over a 10-inch pie pan. Create decorative edge as desired. Refrigerate 30 minutes, or until chilled.

Heat oven to 400 degrees. Drain fruit, reserving syrup. Separate whole slices from cut pieces, and set aside.

Combine whole eggs and citrus syrup in the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the paddle attachment; beat on medium high until pale yellow and fluffy, 5 minutes. Remove from mixer; stir in orange and lemon eighths. Pour into shell; top with reserved fruit slices.

In a small bowl, mix egg yolk and cream. Using a pastry brush, gently brush dough edges with egg-yolk mixture. Place pie on a baking sheet; bake 15 minutes, then reduce heat to 350 degrees. continue to bake 35 to 40 minutes more, until pie is set.

Let cool completely on a wire rack, 2 to 3 hours.

Serve with whipped cream, lavender or vanilla ice cream, or all on its own.

Pie Dough

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

Combine flour, salt, and sugar in food processor. Add butter; process until mixture resembles coarse meal, about 8 to 10 seconds. For hand method, place dry ingredients in large bowl. Add butter; blend with pastry cutter until mixture resembles coarse meal.

Add ice water in a slow steady stream through feed tube of food processor with machine running, until dough holds together for no longer than 30 seconds. For hand method, mix dough with a wooden spoon, adding water until dough just holds together.

Turn dough onto piece of plastic wrap. Press into flat circle, and wrap it in plastic; refrigerate at least 1 hour. May be frozen, double wrapped in plastic, for several months.
